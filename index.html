Three Big Island

The Philippines is composed of three major islands known as Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao. The largest island is Luzon, followed by Mindanao and the Visayas group.

Luzon

Luzon sits at the northern end of the Philippines, and is the country’s largest and most populous island. It’s known for its mountains, beaches and coral reefs, and is home to Manila, the national capital.

Visayas

Visayas is one of the Philippines' 3 main island groups. In Central Visayas, Cebu City’s Spanish colonial architecture includes the triangular Fort San Pedro and the Basilica del Santo Niño.

Mindanao

Mindanao encompasses the large island of Mindanao plus smaller islands in the southern Philippines. In its largest city, Davao, the Philippine Eagle Centre and Davao Crocodile Park showcase local wildlife

Philipines Culture

The Philippines is a culture in which East meets West. The Filipino people have a distinct Asian background, with a strong Western tradition. The modern Filipino culture developed through influence from Chineased traders, Spanish conquistadors, and American rulers.

Filipino Hospitality

Filipino hospitality, or “magiliw na pagtanggap,” is a cultural practice passed down from generation to generation. Basically, it refers to the warm and welcoming nature of the Filipino people towards their guests. It is deeply ingrained in the Philippines' culture to treat visitors with utmost respect and generosity.

Boost tourism industry

Filipinos’ warm and welcoming nature has helped make the Philippines a top tourist destination in Asia. The hospitality industry increases the country’s economy and provides jobs for many Filipinos.

Promotes social connection

Filipino hospitality promotes social harmony by encouraging cooperation, respect, and empathy among individuals and groups.

Enhances personal and professional relationships

Practicing hospitality can lead to stronger personal and professional relationships, as it involves treating others with kindness, respect, and generosity.

Foreign Feedback

Foreigners living in the Philippines contribute to the country's cultural diversity and economy. They often bring unique perspectives and skills, contributing positively to various sectors such as tourism, business, and education. Many expatriates choose the Philippines for its beautiful landscapes, friendly locals, and lower cost of living compared to their home countries.
